About Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice Terraces are ancient terraces carved into the mountains of Banaue, Ifugao, in the Philippines. Constructed by the ancestors of the Igorot people over 2,000 years ago, these terraces are often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World."

The terraces showcase the ingenuity of the indigenous people, who sculpted the mountainsides to create a sustainable agricultural system. Banaue Rice Terraces are not only a cultural treasure but also a testament to the harmony between humanity and nature.

Exploring the Terraces

Discover the wonders of Banaue Rice Terraces by:

  • Viewing Points: Visit designated viewpoints to appreciate the panoramic beauty of the terraces and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Heritage Villages: Explore nearby villages to learn about the rich cultural heritage of the Ifugao people, known for their craftsmanship and traditional practices.
  • Hiking Adventures: Embark on hiking trails that lead to different levels of the terraces, offering a closer look at the agricultural marvel and breathtaking scenery.
  • Local Markets: Experience local life by visiting markets where you can interact with residents, sample local delicacies, and purchase handmade crafts.
