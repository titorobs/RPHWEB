About Tubbataha Reef

Tubbataha Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a protected marine area located in the Sulu Sea. It is renowned for its exceptional biodiversity and pristine coral reefs.

Comprising two atolls, Tubbataha Reef is a haven for marine life, including various species of fish, sharks, turtles, and coral formations. Divers from around the world are drawn to its crystal-clear waters and vibrant underwater landscapes.

Exploring the Reef

Discover the wonders of Tubbataha Reef by:

  • Diving Expeditions: Join diving expeditions to explore the diverse marine life and coral formations in the heart of Tubbataha.
  • Wildlife Encounters: Witness incredible wildlife encounters, including sightings of hammerhead sharks, manta rays, and various species of sea turtles.
  • Conservation Efforts: Learn about the ongoing conservation efforts to preserve the delicate ecosystem of Tubbataha Reef and contribute to its protection.
  • Research Opportunities: Support scientific research initiatives focused on marine conservation and the study of Tubbataha's unique biodiversity.
