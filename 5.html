About Taal Volcano

Taal Volcano is a large caldera filled by Taal Lake in the Philippines. Located in the province of Batangas, approximately 50 kilometers south of Manila, the volcano is renowned for its "perfect cone" due to its symmetric conical shape.

The caldera was formed by prehistoric eruptions between 140,000 and 5,380 BP. Taal Volcano is the second most active volcano in the country, with 38 recorded historical eruptions, all concentrated on Volcano Island in the middle of Taal Lake.

Exploring the Volcano

Discover the wonders of Taal Volcano by:

  • Volcano Island: Take a boat ride to Volcano Island and trek to the crater for a breathtaking view of the volcanic landscape and Taal Lake.
  • Historical Significance: Learn about the historical eruptions and geological significance of Taal Volcano, which has shaped the surrounding landscapes.
  • Scenic Boat Tours: Enjoy scenic boat tours on Taal Lake, offering picturesque views of the volcano and its surroundings.
  • Local Culture: Immerse yourself in the local culture of Batangas and explore nearby communities with unique traditions and heritage.
